## 📰 Summary

Iran has rejected claims by US President Donald Trump that it requested a ceasefire, calling the statement "false and baseless". The development comes amid escalating conflict in the Middle East, with continued military strikes, rising casualties, and global concerns over oil supply and regional stability.

---

## ❗ Key Developments

- Iran's foreign ministry denied that it had asked for a ceasefire, contradicting statements made by Donald Trump.
- Trump claimed that Iran’s "new regime president" had reached out, though he did not clarify who he meant.
- The US indicated it would only consider a ceasefire once the Strait of Hormuz is "open, free, and clear".
- Ongoing strikes have been reported across Iran and Israel.
- The death toll in Lebanon has surpassed 1,300.

---

## 🌍 International Response

In the UK, Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced plans to host a meeting with multiple countries to address security concerns surrounding the Strait of Hormuz.

The situation has drawn global attention due to the strategic importance of the region, particularly for oil transportation.

---

## 🛢️ Economic Impact

- Oil prices briefly dropped below $100 per barrel amid uncertainty.
- الأسواق العالمية remain sensitive to developments in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical route for global energy supplies.

---

## ⚠️ Ongoing Conflict

The situation remains fluid, with continued military activity reported across the region. Analysts warn that further escalation could have significant geopolitical and economic consequences.

---

## 📌 Context

The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most important النفط shipping lanes, and any disruption can impact global energy markets.

This latest dispute highlights the ongoing tensions between the United States and Iran, with conflicting narratives adding to uncertainty.
